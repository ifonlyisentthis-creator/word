How it works

The Afterword protocol, step by step.

One timer controls every item. Check in before it expires and nothing is released.

1. Set your timer

Choose a check-in cadence. The default is 30 days, adjustable on Pro and Lifetime.

2. Add vault entries

Write messages, add recipients, or record audio. Everything is encrypted on your device.

3. Stay secure

Open Afterword and hold the pulse to reset the timer. Your vault stays locked and private.

4. Protocol executes

If you miss the deadline, Afterword sends or destroys each item based on your instructions.

Security model

Built so even the admin can’t peek.

Client-side encryption

Your device generates a random key for every entry and encrypts the data before upload. The server only sees ciphertext.

Encrypted recipients

Recipient emails are encrypted, so database admins cannot swap addresses without breaking the seal.

HMAC tamper seal

Every message is signed with a device secret. If anything is altered, the system deletes the data instead of sending it.

Audio vault protection

Voice notes are encrypted into noise before storage. Without the key, they are unplayable.

FAQ

Clear answers, no jargon.

Can Afterword read my messages?

No. Messages are encrypted on your device. The server stores only encrypted blobs.

What happens if I check in late?

If the deadline passes, the protocol executes your send or destroy rules. Sent items remain visible for 7 days.

Can I change recipients later?

Yes. Edit any active entry to update recipients or rewrite the message. It re-encrypts on save.

How does the audio vault work?

Audio is recorded, encrypted, and uploaded as noise. Lifetime users have a 10-minute total time bank.
